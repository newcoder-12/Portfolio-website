About Me

I’m Nehal Khan, someone who never really saw coding as just lines of text — I saw it as a way to build things that live on the internet. My journey into development began with curiosity, a laptop, and countless nights of “Why isn’t this working?” followed by the satisfying “Finally!” moments.
I started with the basics — HTML and CSS — experimenting with simple pages, colors, layouts, and turning ideas into something visual. That feeling of seeing my first webpage work fueled me. One project led to another, from designing basic UI structures to building a personal portfolio, improving every time I broke and fixed something. I’m not just learning to code — I’m learning to think like a developer. I enjoy solving problems, making designs cleaner, adding little interactions that make a website feel alive. Slowly, I’m expanding into responsive layouts, JavaScript functionality, and building real-world projects that reflect who I am becoming.
I believe growth doesn’t happen overnight. It happens through consistency — one line of code today, a better one tomorrow. When I’m not debugging or styling something pixel-perfect, you’ll probably find me scrolling through designs for inspiration, exploring UI ideas, or planning what to build next. My goal is simple: To become a frontend developer who builds aesthetic, responsive, user-focused web experiences.

This portfolio isn’t just a website —

it’s a reflection of my journey, my improvement, and the developer I am growing into.
